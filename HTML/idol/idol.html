Description

Early Life and Education

Snoop Dogg was born on October 20, 1971, in Long Beach, California, United States. His real name is Calvin Cordozar Broadus Jr. He attended Long Beach Polytechnic High School. During his youth, he developed a strong interest in music and began rapping at a young age. His passion for music later helped him become one of the most famous hip-hop artists in the world.

Career

Snoop Dogg began his music career in the early 1990s. He gained fame after working with Dr. Dre on the album The Chronic in 1992. In 1993, he released his debut album Doggystyle, which became a major success. Throughout his career, he has released many hit songs and albums, acted in films and television shows, and became one of the most influential figures in hip-hop culture.
